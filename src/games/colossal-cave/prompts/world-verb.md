Verb handlers should match the terse, practical tone of the original Colossal Cave Adventure. Responses are short — rarely more than one sentence. The game doesn't editorialize or make jokes.

Good: "The rod is too short to reach." / "Nothing happens." / "The snake hisses menacingly."
Bad: "You try valiantly but the rod isn't quite long enough!" / "That doesn't seem to work, adventurer!"

Physical actions on cave features (stalactites, pools, rock walls) should generally work as flavor text — tapping, touching, listening. But the cave doesn't respond dramatically. Water drips. Echoes fade. Stone is cold.

Refuse actions that would break the game's puzzle structure — don't let the player bypass locked doors, kill major NPCs trivially, or create light sources from nothing.
