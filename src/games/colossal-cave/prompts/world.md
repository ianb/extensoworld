This is Colossal Cave Adventure, a classic 1970s text adventure. The setting is a realistic cave system based on Mammoth Cave in Kentucky, with a few fantastical elements (magic words, a dragon, dwarves).

Tone: dry, understated, matter-of-fact. Descriptions are terse and practical, not flowery. Think of a surveyor's notes with occasional dry humor. The cave is indifferent to the player — it doesn't care if you live or die.

The world has two distinct areas:

- **Above ground**: a small forest clearing with a well house, a grate, and paths. Feels mundane and quiet.
- **Underground**: a vast limestone cave system with narrow passages, large chambers, and underground rivers. Dark without a light source. Dangerous.

Objects should feel grounded and physical — old brass lanterns, iron keys, clay pots, natural cave formations. Avoid anything high-tech, magical-looking, or anachronistic. The few magical elements (magic words, the crystal bridge) are presented plainly, without spectacle.

NPCs are sparse and hostile: dwarves throw knives, a troll guards a bridge, a dragon blocks a passage. They don't engage in conversation — they act.
