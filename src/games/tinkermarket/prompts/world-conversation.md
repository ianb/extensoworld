NPCs are people who happen to work at a market, not market functions who happen to be people.

## They have lives

Every NPC has a job, but they also have opinions, irritations, and things they find funny. Grist has views on how people misuse the tools. Tiv has opinions about other vendors on the Row. The Collector has seen a lot of terrible craftsmanship and remembers specific examples. When generating new conversation responses, let these come through.

An NPC who only talks about their function — "I sell materials," "I buy goods" — is boring. An NPC who mentions that the vendor two stalls down has been undercutting prices on shimmerite by selling an inferior grade, and they can tell because they can literally see through it — that's a person.

## They're busy

NPCs are working while they talk. They sort stock, adjust equipment, examine goods. Narration should show this — they don't stop to face you and deliver a speech. They talk while doing something else.

## They're experts, not teachers

They know their domain cold and talk about it the way experts do — with shorthand, assumptions, and occasional impatience when you ask obvious questions. They don't explain things from first principles unless you really press them. They might point you toward someone who knows more, or tell you to just try it and see.

## Short responses

1-3 sentences. They have things to do. If a topic needs more, they'll highlight a follow-up word so the player can ask more.

## Voice

Each NPC has a distinct voice established by their existing conversation entries. Match it. Don't homogenize them into the same helpful-shopkeeper tone.
<pick num="2">
- A vendor who's been on their feet all day sounds different from one who just opened.
- Someone who's proud of their expertise sounds different from someone who's tired of explaining.
- An NPC who likes the player sounds different from one who's sizing them up.
- Impatience, enthusiasm, distraction, amusement — these are more interesting than helpfulness.
- If an NPC would find a question annoying, let them be annoyed. If they'd find it funny, let them laugh.
- Not every NPC needs to be nice. Some are brusque, some are distracted, some are suspicious of newcomers.
</pick>
