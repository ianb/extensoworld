Created objects must fit in a trans-dimensional marketplace focused on materials and crafting. Descriptions should use the dry, deadpan voice — one aside at most, treating strangeness as unremarkable. Match the tone of the zone the entity is being created in.

## Secrets are essential

Every created entity should have a `secret` property. Secrets describe hidden interactive potential — what happens when the player experiments, what this object can do that isn't obvious, how it connects to other things. Secrets drive the verb fallback system: when a player tries something unexpected, the secret tells the AI how to respond.

Good secrets for this world:
- Materials that react to other specific materials (heat, light, vibration, proximity)
- Tools that have an unintended secondary use
- Vendors who know something they're not advertising
- Objects that change state under specific conditions
- Connections between items in different parts of the market

Secrets should suggest interactions, not just store trivia. "This powder ignites when mixed with coppervine shavings" is actionable. "This powder was mined on a distant world" is not.

## Materials and goods

Materials should have specific, tangible properties that suggest what they can do. Avoid vague descriptions. "A powder that feels heavier the longer you hold it" tells you something. "A mysterious substance" tells you nothing.

Think about what each material's properties imply for crafting. If it conducts, what could you build with it? If it resists shaping, what would happen if you forced it? If it changes temperature, what does that enable?

## Stalls and vendors

Vendors come from different dimensions — their biology and sensibilities vary, but they're all here to do business. Give them specialties rooted in where they're from. A vendor from a high-gravity world sells dense materials. A vendor who perceives magnetic fields sells things organized by polarity instead of by type.

## Crafting potential

New materials should suggest combinations. If you create a new material, its secret should hint at what it combines with. The player should look at new things and wonder: what can I make with this?

## What to avoid

Fantasy clichés (enchanted swords, magical scrolls, prophecies). Combat equipment. Anything that feels generic or templated. The strangeness comes from the diversity of dimensions and the specificity of the goods, not from magic or mysticism.
